1. Executive Summary

Midguard Academy is a BJJ & Kickboxing gym currently operating at 4551 8th St S, Wisconsin Rapids, WI with 29 students. The academy is relocating to 151 Market Ave, Port Edwards, WI on June 15, 2026. The goal is to grow to 75+ students within 12 months of the grand opening.

Key findings:

  • The Wisconsin Rapids‚ÄìMarshfield micro area has a combined population of ~74,000, with the broader draw area (including Stevens Point/Plover at ~41,000) totaling 115,000+ people
  • There is no dedicated BJJ gym in the Wisconsin Rapids/Port Edwards area ‚Äî the only martial arts competitor is Park Institute of Taekwondo (est. 1979), which focuses on traditional Taekwondo
  • Midguard's new pricing ($75/mo single, $105/mo multi) is competitive and appropriately positioned against regional comps ($75‚Äì$85/mo for BJJ-only; $85‚Äì$130+ for multi-discipline)
  • Referrals are the #1 channel ‚Äî formalizing a referral program alone could add 15-20 students in Year 1
  • The new location move is a massive marketing opportunity ‚Äî a grand opening event could generate 10-15 new sign-ups in a single weekend

Bottom line: Going from 29 to 75 requires adding ~46 students. At a realistic pace of 4-6 new students/month (with 2-3% monthly churn), this is achievable in 10-14 months with systematic execution.

5. Referral Program Design

Since referrals are Midguard's proven #1 channel, this needs to be systematic, not casual.

5.1 "Bring a Friend" Referral Program

Program Name: "The Midguard Alliance"

How it works:

  • Every current member gets a personal referral code/card (physical + digital)
  • When a referred friend signs up for a paid membership:
    • Referrer gets: 1 free month of training (or $50 credit toward merch/private lessons)
    • New member gets: 50% off their first month ($37.50 instead of $75)
  • Bonus tiers:
    • 3 referrals in a year = Free Roll Local rash guard ($60+ value)
    • 5 referrals in a year = 1 free month + "Midguard Ambassador" recognition
    • 10 referrals = Free annual membership

5.2 Implementation

Step 1: Launch materials

  • Design referral cards (business card size) with unique codes
  • Create a simple tracking spreadsheet or use GymDesk's referral tracking
  • Print 10 cards per current member

Step 2: Launch announcement

  • Announce at classes, post on social media, email all members
  • Frame it as: "Help us fill the new space ‚Äî and earn free training"

Step 3: Make it easy

  • Members should be able to text their referral link to friends
  • New member sign-up form should ask "Who referred you?"
  • Track and celebrate referrals publicly (whiteboard in gym)

Step 4: Timing

  • Launch referral program 4 weeks before grand opening (mid-May 2026)
  • Create urgency: "Founding Member" rates for anyone who signs up before June 15

5.3 "Founding Member" Campaign

For the new location, create a limited-time offer:

  • Founding Members who sign up before June 15, 2026 get:
    • Locked-in rate of $65/mo (single) or $95/mo (multi) ‚Äî for as long as they stay
    • Free Roll Local t-shirt
    • Name on the "Founding Members" wall in the new gym
  • Limited to first 50 members (creates scarcity)
  • This gives existing members an incentive to lock in lower rates AND bring friends

6. Retention Strategy

The martial arts industry averages 60-80% annual retention (20-40% churn). Midguard should target 85%+ annual retention (‚â§15% churn).

6.1 The First 90 Days (Critical Window)

Most students who quit do so within their first 90 days. Combat this with:

Week 1: Welcome Protocol

  • Personal introduction to all instructors and 2-3 "buddy" members
  • Text from Josh: "Great having you in class tonight! Any questions, I'm here."
  • Gift: Midguard t-shirt or water bottle with name

Week 2-4: Check-in Cadence

  • Instructor checks in after every class ("How are you feeling? Any questions?")
  • If a student misses 2 consecutive classes ‚Üí personal text: "We missed you! Everything okay?"

Month 2-3: Integration

  • Invite to social events (open mat, BBQ, team outing)
  • Encourage participation in partner drills/sparring (builds social bonds)
  • First goal-setting conversation: "Where do you want to be in 6 months?"

6.2 Ongoing Retention Tactics

Belt/Rank Progression:

  • Formal belt promotion ceremonies (BJJ)
  • Progress tracking visible to students (stripes, skill checklists)
  • Celebrate every promotion on social media

Community Building:

  • Monthly social events (BBQ, movie night, open mat sessions)
  • Annual team competition (even inter-gym friendly)
  • Private Facebook group or WhatsApp group for members
  • Holiday parties, summer BBQs, family days

Attendance Tracking:

  • Use GymDesk to monitor attendance patterns
  • If attendance drops below 2x/week for 2 weeks ‚Üí automated check-in message
  • Quarterly "perfect attendance" recognition

Family Integration:

  • Parents who watch kids' classes should be invited to try adult classes
  • Family events that get everyone on the mat
  • Sibling discounts make it easy to add family members

6.3 Exit Interviews

When someone does cancel:

  • Personal call from Josh (not a text or email)
  • Ask: "What could we have done differently?"
  • Offer a "pause" option (hold membership for 1-3 months) instead of cancel
  • Keep them on email list for win-back campaigns
